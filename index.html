Screen Time

How much is too much?

Introduction

Technology is beautiful. The internet has defined a new age of humanity: the information age. Today we are connected in ways unlike those offered to any other generation. We can collaborate, learn, and play together without regard to where we physically are in the real world. Information travels at the speed of light. We have created an entirely new universe: the digital world, a world that has given us new ways to innovate and push forward. However, all of this is not without consequences. At the same time that technology helps us, it also distracts us. When constantly bombarded with an ebbing and flowing vortex of information that is the internet, it becomes increasingly difficult to focus our attention on one particular thing and get anything meaningful done. These distractions are why the average attention span of Gen Z is just eight seconds (McSpadden, 2015). Technology is a double-edged sword. The question is, how much time should children be spending in this digital world? Here are three possibilities:

Highly limited Screen Time

Some people would say that children should stay away from technology; however, this is not a wise choice. Technology is central in today's digital world, and the jobs of the future will center around it. Cybersecurity, Computer Programming, Information Technology, Robotics, Engineering, and many besides are examples of jobs in technology. Children need to be able to learn how to use technology in order to be ready for the future. Keeping technology away from people about to live in this digital world will leave them unprepared when they become adults.

No limits

The other extreme option for this issue would be to let children completely self-manage their screen time.
